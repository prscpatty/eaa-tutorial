🎤 SPEAKER NOTES — Slide 6
"Here's a real scenario inspired by ATEC's early days — before the IoT technology existed. Field agents visited households monthly, wrote in notebooks, reported via WhatsApp, calculated credits in Excel, paid in cash. That's the Baseline. Now ATEC wants to automate everything. That's the Target. Your job: what are the gaps? 4 minutes, same groups."

Prompt stuck groups: "What needs to EXIST in the target that doesn't exist right now?"

Challenge fast groups: "What breaks first if ATEC just bought IoT stoves tomorrow without fixing anything else?"

Debrief: Ask each group for one gap, write them live. Then group by category and reveal the name "Gap Analysis".

"Organisations don't fail digital transformations because they can't afford the tech. They fail because they didn't map the gaps first."

🛵 UBER EATS EDITION
🎤 SPEAKER NOTES — Slide 8
"Before we do this — quick ATEC example. If the architects only asked 'What does ATEC do?' and forgot to ask 'Who are the users?' — they might have built a system that doesn't work for rural households with basic phones. Zachman forces you to ask all six. Now let's apply them to something you all know — Uber Eats. First good answer wins."

Expected answers: WHAT → food orders, menus, delivery data  |  HOW → order → confirm → pick up → deliver  |  WHERE → GPS zones, restaurant locations  |  WHO → customers, restaurants, drivers  |  WHEN → order timing, peak hours  |  WHY → convenient food delivery at scale

If answers are slow — call on someone by name using their tag. Keep pace snappy.

"If Uber Eats skipped 'When' — they'd miss that their system needs to handle massive load spikes at lunch and dinner. That's a completely different infrastructure problem."
